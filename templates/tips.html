TIPS, TRICKS & SAFETY
Beginners guide to biking
GEAR UP
First step to experience the city!
Most hotels in Amsterdam have their own bikes they rent to visitors. Additionally, there are many bike shops scattered throughout the city where you can rent bikes for around 10 euros a day. You can also get kids bikes as well as bikes with child seats and tandem bikes.

*PS: A raincoat will be handy more often than you think!*

GENERAL TIPS
A guide for those unfamiliar with the city
*Tram rails are everywhere!* Make sure to cross them at an angle so your bike doesn't get stuck in there.

Just a heads up, scooters are allowed on some bike paths. It can be scary to hear the engine speeding right behind you but remember to *stay on the right* and give them space to go by and you'll be fine.

With the city of bikes comes the city of thieves, make sure to *lock up your bike securely* whenever you park.

Pedestrians can sometimes be unpredictable so keep an eye out for anyone ahead who might suddenly try to cross the bike path.
