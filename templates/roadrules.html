RULES OF THE ROAD
Things to remember
TRIANGLES
What do they mean?
While biking, you will notice some funny triangles drawn on the path at intersections. *This is to illustrate who has right of way.* If it faces away from you, you have right of way to keep biking. However, if it points to you, you have to brake and give way to passing bikes
*Always, always, always stay on the right!* Make sure there is enough space on your left for faster cyclists to overtake. The worst mistake you can make is to bike in the middle of the bike path.
PARKING
Where and how to park correctly
You will come to find that parking is a bit of an issue in Amsterdam. However, you are usually able to park your bike right infront of your destination.

Here are some things to note:
1. *Park in designated racks* or painted boxes, or look at where bikes are clustered and park there (usually fences/walls/bridges)

2. *Don't block the sidewalk* or park right infront of a shop window

3. The signs that say "P Fiets in de rekken max 14 degen" mean you have to park your bike in the racks (or it will be removed) and you cannot leave it longer than 14 days
